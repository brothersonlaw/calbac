    Retrograde extrapolation

    Estimate BAC at the time of driving from a later chemical test, assuming the subject was post-absorptive (past peak) the entire time. The tool adds back the alcohol eliminated between driving and the test: BACdriving = BACtest + β · hours — where β (beta) is the elimination rate, i.e. how much BAC the body clears per hour. If the subject was still absorbing, this overstates the driving BAC — the rising-BAC defense.

    Why this cuts both ways

    • For the prosecution: if truly post-absorptive, driving BAC was higher than the test.
    • For the defense: retrograde is only valid post-peak. Absent a known drinking pattern, no one can say which phase the driver was in — see the Cross-Exam tab.
    • The wider the time gap and the more uncertain β, the less reliable the number.

    Breath test & blood:breath ratio

    Breath instruments assume a fixed 2100:1 blood-to-breath ratio. Real ratios vary (~1100:1 to 4200:1). A lower personal ratio means the machine over-reports. Breathing pattern and breath temperature bias the reading further.
